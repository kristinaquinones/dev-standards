# GTM Launch Planning

## Overview

GTM (go-to-market) launch planning helps you prepare for and execute a successful product launch. This document covers frameworks for planning launches, coordinating activities, and measuring success. For product release planning, see [Release Planning](../product-docs/release-planning.md).

## GTM Launch Framework

### What is a GTM Launch?

A GTM launch is how you introduce your product to the market. It includes positioning, messaging, channel activation, and coordination with product release. A successful launch creates awareness, generates interest, and drives initial adoption.

### Launch Planning Process

**Step 1: Define Launch Goals**
- What are you trying to achieve with this launch?
- Set specific, measurable goals
- Use [SMART goals framework](../product-docs/metrics-success-criteria.md#smart-goals-framework) for goal setting

**Step 2: Identify Target Audience**
- Who are you launching to?
- Use [user research](../product-docs/user-research-discovery.md) to understand your audience
- Segment audiences if launching to multiple groups

**Step 3: Develop Positioning and Messaging**
- Define how you'll position the product
- Create messaging for launch
- See [Positioning & Messaging](positioning-messaging.md) for frameworks

**Step 4: Select Launch Channels**
- Choose channels to reach your target audience
- Plan channel-specific activities
- See [Channel Strategy](channel-strategy.md) for channel selection

**Step 5: Create Launch Timeline**
- Sequence launch activities
- Coordinate with product release
- Plan pre-launch, launch, and post-launch activities

**Step 6: Set Up Measurement**
- Define success metrics
- Set up tracking and analytics
- Plan for post-launch review
- See [Analytics Strategy](../measurement-docs/analytics-strategy.md) for measurement

## Launch Sequence

### Launch Timeline Framework

**T-4 Weeks: Pre-Launch Preparation**
- Finalize positioning and messaging
- Prepare all launch content
- Set up channels and measurement
- Begin soft outreach to key contacts

**T-2 Weeks: Pre-Launch Activities**
- Share with early users or beta testers
- Gather testimonials or case studies
- Finalize all launch materials
- Test all systems and processes

**T-1 Week: Launch Countdown**
- Final content review
- Confirm all channel activities
- Brief team on launch plan
- Prepare for launch day

**Launch Day: Execution**
- Execute launch activities across channels
- Monitor channels for questions or issues
- Track initial metrics
- Be available to respond quickly

**T+1 Week: Post-Launch Review**
- Review launch metrics
- Gather feedback from early users
- Identify what worked and what didn't
- Plan follow-up activities

### Launch Activities by Channel

**Owned Channels:**
- Website updates
- Email to existing contacts
- Blog posts or announcements
- Product updates or changelogs

**Earned Channels:**
- Press outreach (if applicable)
- Community announcements
- Influencer or partner outreach
- Word-of-mouth activation

**Paid Channels:**
- Paid advertising campaigns
- Sponsored content
- Promoted posts or announcements

**Note:** Specific activities depend on your channel strategy and resources.

## Post-Launch Activities

### Immediate Follow-Up (First Week)

- Monitor channels for questions and feedback
- Respond to early users quickly
- Track initial metrics and engagement
- Address any issues that arise

### Review and Learn (First Month)

- Review launch metrics against goals
- Gather qualitative feedback
- Identify what worked well
- Identify what needs improvement

### Iterate and Improve

- Make adjustments based on learnings
- Optimize messaging based on feedback
- Adjust channel mix if needed
- Plan ongoing marketing activities

## Launch Metrics

### Awareness Metrics

- Reach: How many people saw your launch?
- Impressions: How many times was your message seen?
- Brand searches: Are people searching for your product?

### Engagement Metrics

- Website traffic: How many people visited?
- Content engagement: How did people interact with content?
- Social engagement: How did people engage on social channels?

### Conversion Metrics

- Signups or trials: How many people signed up?
- Conversions: How many took desired actions?
- Early adoption: How many became active users?

### Channel Performance

- Performance by channel: Which channels performed best?
- Cost per acquisition: What did it cost to acquire users?
- Channel ROI: Which channels provided best return?

**Measurement Framework:**
- Set baseline metrics before launch
- Track metrics during launch
- Compare post-launch to baseline
- Use [analytics strategy](../measurement-docs/analytics-strategy.md) for measurement

## Launch Best Practices

- **Align with Product Release**: Coordinate GTM launch with product release
- **Start Early**: Begin planning well before launch date
- **Test Everything**: Test messaging, content, and channels before launch
- **Be Flexible**: Be ready to adjust based on feedback and data
- **Measure Everything**: Track metrics to understand what worked
- **Follow Up**: Don't stop after launch day; continue engaging users

## Common Pitfalls

**Poor Coordination:**
- GTM launch and product release not aligned
- Team not coordinated on timing
- Solution: Plan together and align timelines

**Unclear Messaging:**
- Messaging not clear or tested
- Different channels have conflicting messages
- Solution: Develop and test messaging early

**Channel Overload:**
- Trying to launch on too many channels
- Not executing any channel well
- Solution: Focus on 1-3 channels for launch

**No Measurement:**
- Not tracking launch performance
- Can't learn from launch
- Solution: Set up measurement before launch

**No Follow-Up:**
- Stopping after launch day
- Not engaging with early users
- Solution: Plan post-launch activities
